The Basic Components

Starting off can be tough! For this lesson we will learn the fundamentals of the guitar.

To start with we will take a look at the components of the guitar so that you can familiarize yourself with the main parts, what they are used for and were to find them.

Just say, "Open Help Center" at any time to open the voice command menu.

TheGuitars Head Stock

The head stock can be found at the top of the guitar. Its purpose is to stock the tuning pegs or the machine heads that the strings hold into. The manufacturers logo can be found here too. Headstocks can vary in design depending on the manufacturer. Some cut through the headstock to fit tuning pegs, some keep the solid shape and fit machine heads (seen in the photograph).

The Machine Heads

These can be found on the headstock. They are used to holds the strings tightly in place and with a turn in either direction can tune your guitar to the required note. Tuning pegs come in various designs and dynamics, composing of either metal or nylon, depending on the type of guitar you have and its manufacturer, but they all have the same purpose - to tune your guitar.

The Nut (Top Bridge)

The Nut is a small piece of hard material that supports the strings at the end of the headstock. The nut marks one end of the string, sets the spacing of the strings across the neck of the guitar evenly and usually holds the strings at the correct height from the fingerboard. It is essential that the strings are kept in place, the strings can become misplaced if tuned too far out.

Fingerboard

The Fingerboard is usually a long thin strip of wood that runs from the headstock down and past the body of the guitar. Depending on the type of guitar you have depicts the way it is constructed. A Classical guitar will have a flat fingerboard, an acoustic will have a slight bezel to it and an electric guitar has a much thinner and highly lacquered finish to it. The strings run over the fingerboard, between the nut and bridge

Frets

Frets are the horizontal metal strips that run down the fingerboard, their purpose is to add different pitches to any open string once it is played. Pushing down between any two frets is the correct way to play and this is called fretting, more on this later. These can wear down over time, and can cause the sound to be a little less than desired. If caught early this is relatively cheap to have fixed by a professional.

Bridge

The Bridge can always be found below the sound hole on an acoustic guitar, or below the pick ups, on an electric guitar. The main purpose is to fix the strings in place.

Attached to the Bridge is an important component called the Saddle, which acts like the nut and keeps the strings in place. In the picture above there is white plastic - that is the saddle, the black wood surrounding it is called the Bridge.
