Standard Tuning

Tuning your guitar correctly is so important before playing any song. In this lesson we will learn how to tune any Six stringed guitar using Standard Tuning.

For the majority of songs, Standard Tuning is used to tune the guitar to a natural E.This this means that both, the bottom and lightest string (String 1) is tuned to E and the top bass string (string 6) is also tuned to E - You will find out by playing with the tuner below that these two notes are at different pitches.

The order of the strings run in sequential order from strings 1 - 6 as E, A, D, G, B, E.

A good way to remember this is to remember the phrase, " Every Amateur Does Get Better Eventually."

For all voice commands for this website, just say, "Open Help Center" at any time to open the Help Box.

Tuning your Guitar

Using the tuner above, you can either click on a guitar note, E, A, D, G, B, E, or activate using your own voice, saying, "play," before the note you require, this will activate that note and the string on both guitar's will light up too.

The Strings will highlight only one time, so try and remember instead of relying on the tuner. The aim here is to play the note on the tuner, then on your guitar loosen or tighten your guitars tuning peg to make the exact same string on your guitar sound like the note being played as closely or as exact as possible.

Getting this right will take some time to master, it can be so frustrating too, but don't give up!

The fretboard is an important component on most stringed instruments. It is a thin, long strip of wood, that is laminated to the front of the neck of a string instrument. The string's run over the fingerboard, between the nut and the Bridge.
