Chord Lesson

Every song has chords in it, so it is important to learn the basics. This page will explain two different types of chords, open chords and barre chords.
For all voice commands for this website, just say, "What to say" at any time to open the Help Box.

The quickest way of learning chords, is to start at A. Chords change just like our own alphabet from A through to G (they don't go any further than G). Learn the Open Chords first, there are many videos online and many different techniques to influence you. Then move onto the Barre Chords, these chords add much more depth to the chords you play.

The Open Chord

The open chord is perhaps the easiest of all chord types to play. Strings are fretted by your finger tips hitting the strings on certain frets. Each chord is a combination of notes, when played together they play a richer version of another note.

The Barre Chord

The Barre Chord is perhaps one of the most difficult chords to play for a beginner. The player has to play the E major chord with the middle, ring and the little finger whilst placing their whole index finger right across the fretboard.

The guitar is one of the most evocative instruments in the world. It features in music as diverse as heavy metal, blues, indie and flamenco, as well as Indian classical music, village music making in Papua New Guinea and carnival in Brazil.
