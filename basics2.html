How to hold a Guitar

This page explains how to hold a guitar correctly so that you can reach around with your fingertips to play the strings effortlessly, be prepared this is not an easy task, your wrists will ache and so will your finger tips. Remember to rest but more importantly keep picking your guitar up and practicing, the aches will disappear after a while and you'll never ache again after that initial period.
For all voice commands for this website, just say, "What to say" at any time to open the Help Box.

How to sit with a Guitar.

This image explains how you should comfortably hold your guitar whilst being seated. Its a good idea to learn this way in the beginning as playing stood up can accidentally make your guitar slip whilst playing. The important thing is to relax, sit with the guitar so that you are comfortable and are enjoying playing it without causing any strains.

How to hold the back of the neck.

When holding your guitar its important to cradle the guitar neck rather than to hold tightly. Positioning the thumb around the neck, just before the fretboard whilst the base of your fingers sits on the other side of the fretboard. This should give enough of an arch so that you can reach for any chord you wish to play.

How to arch over the Fretboard

Once you are in a position were you have the correct thumb placement and palm cradling is present, check that your index an middle finger can reach the top three bass strings and your remaining fingers can easily reach the remaining strings. Its best to practice this at the top of the fret board where it is thinner and easier to do.
